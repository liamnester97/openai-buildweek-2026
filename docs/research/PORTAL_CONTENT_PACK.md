# Portal Content Pack

Status: Stage 0 draft. Human science/content approval is required before a claim becomes game text.

## Approved framing

- Time and place: a fictional Late Jurassic floodplain inspired by Morrison Formation ecosystems in the western United States.
- Museum framing: an original, fictional nighttime museum. It is not affiliated with or endorsed by the Denver Museum of Nature & Science.
- Memory framing: the event is a fictional museum echo. It does not claim a shared history for real specimens.
- Playable form: young Stegosaurus.

## Required Language Boundaries

The game may say:

- "This fictional memory is inspired by Late Jurassic ecosystems."
- "The tracks support an interpretation that the predator first followed smaller prey."
- "The protected depression may explain why the Stegosaurus held the route."

The game must not say:

- "This is what happened to real museum specimens."
- "Allosaurus hunted in packs."
- "Stegosaurus nesting behavior is established by this scene."
- "The real museum display is incorrect."

## Mini-Game Learning Objectives

- Learn the Body: distinguish embodiment and sensory perspective from scientific certainty.
- Read the Living Tracks: infer a predator's initial route from aligned traces.
- Protect the Rise: distinguish an observed protected area from a definitive nesting claim.
- Reach High Ground: connect environmental change to constrained movement and rapid burial as a fictional scenario.
