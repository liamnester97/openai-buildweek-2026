# Floodplain Memory Player Journey

## One-minute promise

At 12:03 a.m., the visitor enters an original, fictional natural-history gallery. A glowing reconstructed scene signals that it can be entered. The visitor crosses the threshold, becomes a young Stegosaurus, and receives both a motion path and a simplified-control path.

## Playable chapter

1. Inspect the Allosaurus-versus-Stegosaurus exhibit and hear the museum's simple predator-prey story.
2. Enter the Floodplain Memory portal. Reduced-motion mode replaces the transformation with a crossfade and a clear form-change announcement.
3. In Fern Edge, learn move, forage, and ground-sense actions. This teaches the Stegosaurus-specific verbs without a fail state.
4. In Track Corridor, follow small-animal movement and observe that the Allosaurus trail initially aligns with smaller prey. Award M1.
5. In Nesting Rise, inspect a protected depression and establish why the Stegosaurus remains near a constrained route. Award M2 as a cautious inference, not a proven historic nest.
6. In Flood Channel, choose an accessible safe route and reach high ground as water and ground instability narrow the corridor. Award M3 and M4.
7. Return to the museum. Compare portal memory with track, sediment, and fragment displays.
8. Build a theory from evidence. Deterministic scoring selects the ending; GPT-5.6 may explain the verified result but may not choose it.

## Accessibility Alternatives

- Keyboard and pointer controls in the museum; keyboard, touch, and large-button simplified controls in the habitat.
- A reduced-motion transition with the same narrative information.
- No required reaction window shorter than three seconds; an untimed/extended-time option is always available.
- Captions and visible directional/vibration indicators for every audio cue.
- No state depends solely on color, animation, or canvas-only interaction.
